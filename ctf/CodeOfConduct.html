CTF Student Activities Code of Conduct

Expectations and guidelines for ECPI cybersecurity student organizations and activities.

This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioral expectations for all students who participate in cybersecurity clubs, CTF teams, and related activities. These standards help us maintain a professional, inclusive, and collaborative environment where everyone can learn and contribute.

Community and conduct

1. Purpose

The purpose of this Code of Conduct is to guide behavior within the ECPI Greensboro cybersecurity community. Our goal is to create an environment where everyone feels welcome, supported, and encouraged to grow, regardless of experience level or background.

All students deserve a respectful, safe, and professional learning space. This document outlines the expectations for participation, as well as procedures for addressing misconduct.

2. Community Principles

Cybersecurity is collaborative by nature. Our success depends on how we treat each other, how we share knowledge, and how we support our teammates.

Encourage others when they contribute. Celebrate learning moments. Speak up if harmful behavior occurs. Every student is responsible for sustaining a positive culture.

3. Expected Behavior

All participants are expected to:

  • Act with professionalism, honesty, and respect.
  • Communicate clearly and constructively.
  • Avoid harassment, discrimination, or hostile behavior.
  • Be aware of how your actions affect others.
  • Report concerning behavior to faculty or team leaders.

4. Unacceptable Behavior

The following actions violate community expectations and will not be tolerated:

  • Harassment, discrimination, or hate speech.
  • Violence, threats, or intimidation.
  • Personal insults, bullying, or targeted negative behavior.
  • Sharing explicit, violent, or inappropriate content.
  • Unauthorized access to systems, accounts, or networks.
  • Invasion of privacy or doxxing.
  • Deliberate disruption of learning environments or events.
  • Theft, vandalism, or destruction of property.

5. Consequences of Violations

Participants asked to stop unacceptable behavior must comply immediately. Depending on severity, consequences may include:

  • Verbal or written warnings
  • Removal from an event or activity
  • Referral to university administration for disciplinary action
  • Permanent removal from student organizations or teams

6. Reporting Concerns

Any student who experiences or witnesses misconduct should report the issue promptly to:

  • The Student Organization Advisor
  • A faculty member or Campus Director

Reports will be handled respectfully and confidentially to the extent possible.
