CTF Training Overview

A mix of Q&A, collaborative practice, and remote work designed to build deep, repeatable skills.

Training is where you experiment, break things, and learn from the process. The goal is not perfection; the goal is progress. These sessions are structured to help you ask better questions, learn tools efficiently, and become a teammate others can rely on.

Training Philosophy

Have fun, learn naturally, and make steady progress over time.

Q&A Sessions

Q&A sessions are dedicated time to bring questions about concepts, tools, or challenges that are confusing or frustrating. The group works through them together.

  • Bring specific problems, screenshots, or error messages.
  • If the group cannot answer, we walk through how to research: documentation, search engines, AI tools, and writeups.
  • Focus is on understanding why something works, not just copying a fix.

These sessions are perfect for taking something from ‚ÄúI kind of get it‚Äù to ‚ÄúI can explain this to someone else.‚Äù

In-Person Trainings

In-person training is about collaboration and community. This is where you share ideas, test approaches together, and see how others think through problems.

  • Short mini-lessons on tools, categories, or techniques.
  • Paired or small-group problem solving.
  • Live walkthroughs of example challenges.

Heavy solo ‚Äúgrinding‚Äù is better suited to remote practice. In-person time is best used to share strategies, ask questions out loud, and build team chemistry.

Remote Trainings

Remote training modules are self-paced exercises you complete on your own. They‚Äôre focused on developing independence, persistence, and comfort with core tools.

  • Strengthen fundamentals like Linux, scripting, and networking.
  • Work through beginner and intermediate CTF-style challenges.
  • Rebuild skills regularly instead of cramming before competitions.
